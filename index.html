Top 4 Visa Options for Working Abroad

Choosing whether to move abroad for employment or not is a very difficult decision. You may be a recent graduate looking for great experiences or an experienced professional looking to expand your horizons. Moving abroad opens up doors to unlimited financial opportunities. Also, you will gain new skills that will help boost your career prospects.

Apart from these, moving abroad will allow you to experience different cultures, improve your communication & social skills and develop skills to adapt to any environment. Many countries are now offering many incentives to work in their country. Here are 4 of the best work visa programs offered across the world.

Federal Skilled Trades (FST) Program – Canada

Canada's Federal Skilled Trades (FST) program aims to invite semi-skilled workers worldwide to live and work in the country.

The candidate must have at least two years of full-time work experience in a skilled occupation relevant to an occupation listed in the National Occupational Classification (NOC) list. To be eligible for this program, you must be fluent in English, have an employment offer from a Canadian employer, have the required work experience, and show sufficient funds. Some of the jobs under the FST program are –

  • - Electrician
  • - Plumber
  • - Aircraft Mechanics
  • - Welders and Fitters
  • - Crane Drivers

Job Seeker Visa – Germany

The Germany Job Seeker Visa allows qualified and skilled professionals to enter the country and look for work opportunities. Through this, the job seeker gets access to the country's job market. The government of Germany is currently facing a huge shortage in the workforce. To fill the gap, they introduced the job seeker visa to attract skilled professionals.

with this visa, the job seeker has six months to find a job. If they successfully get a job offer from a German employer within six months, they can apply for a German work visa.

Singapore Work Visa

While Singapore is a highly expensive country, its thriving economy and vibrant culture have attracted many foreign professionals to come and work in the country.

The work visa, or work pass, permits you to work in the country for a defined period. The applicant's minimum age is set as 18 years, while the maximum age is 50 years.

Here are the types of visas available for work in Singapore:

- Entre Pass: This permit is issued to foreign entrepreneurs who want to start a business venture in Singapore.

- Employment Pass: This permit is for professionals, managers and executive-level people who are likely to earn over 3,600 Singaporean dollars per month and have the required qualifications.

- Personalized Employment Pass: This work permit provides more work flexibility than the Employment pass and will be issued to professionals with a higher salary.

- S pass: This pass is issued to the mid-level workers. To apply for this pass, you must earn at least S$2,200 per month and meet the eligibility criteria.

- Miscellaneous Pass: This type of visa is issued to foreign nationals who intend to work in Singapore for a short period.

Malta Work Visa

While Malta is the smallest country in the European Union, it has one of the highest employment rates. It attracts scores of foreign skilled individuals to work there. People from non-EU nations must apply for the Single Permit Application, which is processed by the employer, sponsoring them to live and work in the country.

To be eligible for the visa, the applicant must have:

  • - A copy of the valid employment offer
  • - Private medical insurance that covers for 12 months
  • - Cover letter from the employer
  • - Position description signed by the employer
  • - A signed Resume showing three years of work experience
